Nextdoor

Duration: 2 weeks | Team: Group of 3 | Project Type: Conceptual

Overview

Project Summary

My group and I collaborated in the research and improvement of a functionality in the application "Nextdoor"

What I did:

  • Competitive Analysis
  • User Research
  • Sketch Mockups
  • Wireframe
  • Clickable Prototype
  • Usability Test

Brief

Product background

Nextdoor is a local social networking service for neighborhoods with 66 million users across 11 countries. The app provides a digital platofrm for users in each 'neighborhood' to communicate with one another.

Project Goals:
  • Improvethe functionality of the 'connect with your neighbors' function
  • Find ways for users to match with neighbors and meet in person based on their hobbies and passions, and create a network
  • Explore the ability for users to create, organize, and participate in in-person events based on their passions
  • Set levels of privacy for the user
  • Find a way to protect under 18 year olds who also want to connect with people

Discovery and Research

What we did and learned:


A competitive analysis was done initially to learn about what Nextdoor is currently doing and the design patterns of existing competitors. This showed us that Nextdoor was not following current design patterns and that the application might be improved in this way.

Next 10 user interviews were conducted. Taking the obervations from each interview, my team put together an affinity map to discover strong trends in the data that we can take actionable steps towards. Through synthesizing the data, we discovered strong trends that users:

  • Make friends through existing friends
  • Make friends through hobbies and interests
  • Join clubs/events through friend's recommendations
  • Feel safer in big groups than by themselves
  • Trust strangers as they learned more information about them and meeting them in person
  • Are selective with what information they share about themselves

Roadblocks and what we overcame:

We all discovered that, as this is was our first group project, we all have a different way of approaching the UX design process. This caused for a lot of insightful debate as to how to approach the design process and how to proceed forward. To overcome this, we each discussed what we had to say and agreed as a team how to move forward. We also had our General Assembly instructor help with what to focus on, and we learned more about how to work as a group from the experience. One thing that we did not do during the user interviews was understand the root reason why someone would want to meet new people. To solve this, we decided to hypothesize the reason using Maslow's hierarchy of needs and test it later during usability testing.
